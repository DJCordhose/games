What is a Game Jam?

  • People with a common interest meet and hack on game ideas together
  • Community based and organized
  • You bring your own ideas, possibly your own technology
  • Everyone is welcome
    • w/ or w/o prior game programming experience
    • w/ or w/o an idea for a gaming
    • w/ or w/o a preference for a framework or technology
  • NO COMPETITION - NO PRICES - NO WORKING AT NIGHT
  • Another Jam: http://www.meatlyjam.com/meatlyjam

Schedule for Friday: Preparation

  • approx. 18:45 - 19:00 Introduction to the Game Jam
  • approx. 19:00 - 20:00 People introduce themselves, their (game) ideas, frameworks they want to learn or even their own frameworks
  • approx. 20:00 - 21:00 Teams form and plans are made based on ideas introduced before, maybe frameworks are introduced to other team members
  • 21:00 End of Event for Friday

Schedule for Saturday: Coding and presentation

  • 10:00 - 18:00: You hack in teams
  • approx. 18:00 - 19:00: Presentation of results
  • 19:00: End of Event

If you do not have any ideas

  • Just join someone who has an idea
  • As suggested here http://www.gamasutra.com/view/feature/2438/how_to_prototype_a_game_in_under_7_.php
    • Choose an idea you can finish quickly (in our case just one day)
    • Create a game around a theme, like "gravity" or "collision" or "genetics"
    • http://rednuht.org/genetic_cars_2/ great example using a genetic algorithm
  • More links about how to proceed
    • http://devmag.org.za/2014/01/08/rapid-game-prototyping-tips-for-programmers/
    • http://gamelogic.co.za/2013/12/06/how-we-made-30-games-in-30-days/

Step 1 (approx. 19:00): Introduce yourself, meet other game devs

  • What are you interested in?
  • Do you have an idea? What is it?
  • What or who do you need?
  • What can you offer?
  • This is also the time for a very short pitch (<< 5 min)

Step 2 (approx. 20:00): Planning

  • Find a team
  • Present, explain relevant technology
  • Make plans for tomorrow
